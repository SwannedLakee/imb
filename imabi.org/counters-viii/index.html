Intermediate Counters

～基

The counter ～基き counts a variety of things that are installed and or placed somewhere. Common things it is used for include dams, elevators, grave(stone)s, wreaths, reactors, CPUs, large machinery, lanterns, etc.


1. この町まちは今年ことし、風力発電機ふうりょくはつでんきを10じゅっ基き設置せっちした。
This town installed ten wind turbine generators this year.

2. ダムを1いっ基き建設けんせつするのに20にじゅう年以上ねんいじょうかかります。
It takes over twenty years to construct one dam.

3. 高性能こうせいのうのCPUシーピーユーを2に基き積つんでいます。
It has two high-performance CPUs loaded.

～滴

The counter ～滴てき is used to count drops of liquid.


4. 水みずが1いっ滴てきも入はいっていないんですよ。
There’s not a single drop of water in it.

5. ラー油ゆを10じゅっ滴てきかけて食たべてみました。
I tried eating with 10 drops of Chinese chili oil on it.

6. 指ゆびにオイルをつけて、頬ほっぺたに馴染なじませてください。
Put the oil on your finger and then thoroughly blend it into your cheeks.

～票

The counter ～票ひょう counts votes. The word for vote also happens to be 票.


7. 新人しんじんの男性候補者だんせいこうほしゃが1いっ票ぴょうも獲得かくとくできなかった。
The rookie male candidate was unable to acquire a single vote.

8. 最終投票さいしゅうとうひょうでは、リオデジャネイロが66ろくじゅうろく票獲得ひょうかくとくした。
In the final vote, Rio de Janeiro acquired 66 votes.

9. 渡辺氏わたなべしが5ご万まん4632よんせんろっぴゃくさんじゅうに票獲得ひょうかくとくしました。
Mr. Watanabe acquired 54,632 votes.

～件

The counter 件けん counts situations/incidents. This can extend to accidents, bugs (IT), cases (of a disease, etc.), inquiries, applications, search results, responses, news articles, etc.


10. 首都圏内しゅとけんないでは、今年中ことしじゅうに人身事故じんしんじこが34さんじゅうよん件発生けんはっせいしています。
In greater Tokyo, there have been 34 accidents resulting in injury/death during this year.

11. 神奈川県かながわけんでも、同様どうようの被害ひがいが20にじゅっ件けんくらい発生はっせいしています。
Even in Kanagawa Prefecture, there have been about 20 similar cases of damage.

12. 検出したバグはすでに100ひゃっ件けんを超こえています。
Bugs detected have already surpassed 100.

～行（ぎょう・こう）

When read as ぎょう, ～行 is used to count lines of text. When read as こう, it is used to count banks. However, the phrase 一行いっこう can also be used to mean “group of people/line of…”

～ぎょう


～こう


13. スペイン国有こくゆうの2に行こうが合併がっぺいすることで合意ごういしました。
Two of Spain’s national-owned banks have agreed to merge.

14. 雷かみなりに驚おどろいた一行いっこうの馬うまが暴走ぼうそうして女一人おんなひとりを踏ふみ殺ころした。
A troupe of horses scared by thunder rampaged and trampled one woman to death.

15. 2に行目ぎょうめの文字数もじすうを教えてください。
Please tell me the number of characters in the second line.

16. 〇〇氏一行まるまるしいっこうが中国東北部ちゅうごくとうほくぶへ視察しさつ（し）に行いきました。
Mr. ##’s entourage went to observe Northeast China.

17. ２００８にせんはち年ねんには、アメリカの金融機関きんゆうきかんのうち少すくなくとも１いっ行こうが破綻はたんしてしまった。
In 2008, of the financial institutions in America, at least one bank went into bankruptcy.

～画

The counter ～画かく counts the number of strokes in a 漢字. It can also be used to count plots/lots of land. For this latter meaning, it is usually seen as 区画.

～かく

～くかく


18. 「ヲ」は3さん画かくで書かきます。
“ヲ” is written with 3 strokes.

19. 韓国かんこくの「韓かん」を17じゅうなな画かくで書かくって知しってる？
Did you know that the “kan” in “Kankoku” is written with 17 strokes?

20. このホテルはバンクーバーの観光地域かんこうちいきから約やく15じゅうご区画離くかくはなれた場所ばしょにあります。
This hotel is at a place 15 blocks removed from the tourist area of Vancouver.

21. 分譲地ぶんじょうちの1いっ（区く）画かくを購入こうにゅうする予定よていです。
I intend to purchase one section of a lot.

～種（類）

The counter ～種しゅ（類るい）counts species/kinds.


22. 支払しはらいプランが3さん種類しゅるいあります。
There are three types of payment plans.

23. この2に種しゅ（類るい）の象ぞうの化石かせきは北海道ほっかいどうでも見みつかっている。
Fossils of these two species of elephant have also been found in Hokkadio.

24. 日本固有にほんこゆうの犬種けんしゅは全部ぜんぶで6ろく種しゅあります。
There is a total of six species of dug unique to Japan.

～脚

The counter ～脚きゃく counts chairs, desks, trays with legs, and seats. It can also be the “pod” in 一脚 (mono-pod).” Outside the home, however, these things frequently counted with ～台 or some other counter.


25. オフィスチェアが｛4よん個こ・4よん脚きゃく｝あります。
There are four office chairs.

26. 折おり畳たたみ式しき（の）椅子いすが｛3さん個こ・3さん脚きゃく｝あります。
There are three fold-up chairs.

27. 鉄製てっせいの机つくえが｛2に台だい・2ふたつ・2に脚きゃく｝あります。
There are two steel desks.

28. 三脚さんきゃくを使つかったことがありますか。
Have you ever used a tripod?

Word Note: In the phrase 三脚, the literal meaning of “leg” for the character 脚 is used.

～着

Depending on the clothing, you will either count it with ～着ちゃく or ～枚.  Suits, dresses, coats, and jackets that cover the whole body are counted with ～着. Shirts, blouses, sweaters, skirts, pants, underwear, casual jackets, one-pieces, etc. are counted with ～枚.


29. 社会人しゃかいじんでもスーツを1いっ着ちゃくしか持もってない人ひとはたくさんいるでしょう。
There are plenty of people who may be working-adults yet have but one suit.

30. 浴衣ゆかたは多分たぶん3さん着持ちゃくもってると思おもいます。
I think I probably have three yukatas.

31. 皆みなさんは、仕事用しごとようのスーツは何着持なんちゃくもってるんですか。
How many business suits does everyone have?

～膳

The counter ～膳ぜん counts bowlfuls of rice or pairs of chopsticks.  


32. 私わたしは3さん膳ぜんのご飯はんを平たいらげた。
I ate up three bowfuls of rice.

33. 箸はしを2に膳ぜんください。
Two (pairs of) chopsticks, please.

34. 主食しゅしょくは、1いち日にち2に膳ぜんのご飯はんと卵たまごや魚さかな、お肉にくや豆腐とうふなど蛋白質たんぱくしつの多おおいものを必かならず摂取せっしゅすることが大切たいせつです。
For one’s staple food, it is important that you always intake two bowlfuls of rice and foods rich in protein such as  eggs, fish, meat, tofu, etc. a day.

～貫

The counter ～貫かん has three usages, two of which are obsolete. The first two involve units of currency and weight that are no longer used today but continue to be used when talking about or speaking as if one were in a period in which these units were in use. Otherwise, it is used to count pieces of sushi. Of course, ～個 is also appropriate. Interestingly enough, nearly half of all speakers treat 1貫 as 2個 rather than 1個. This is because sushi is usually dished out as two pieces. However, this practice itself is called 2貫付け, indicating that traditionally 1貫 refers to 1 piece. Because of this confusion, some speakers will either view each increment as referring to either one or two pieces each.


35. 寿司すしは何貫なんかんぐらい食たべられますか。
How many pieces of sushi can you eat?

36. 1いっ貫かんって何個なんこですか？
How much is 1 kan (of sushi)?

37. 大おおトロ10じゅっ貫かん食たべても1200せんにひゃく円えん！
It’s only 1200 yen even if you have 10 pieces of fat under-belly (of tuna)!

～輪

The counter ～輪りん either counts flowers (in bloom) or the number of wheels (on a vehicle). When counting flowers, some speakers prefer to use ～本 whenever a single plant has more than one bud that blooms.


38. この日午前ひごぜん11じゅういち時じごろ、花はなが5ご輪咲りんさいているのを確認かくにんしました。
Today at around 11 AM, (I/we) verified that five flowers have bloomed.

39. 思おもい返かえせば、小ちいさいころに確たしかに三輪車さんりんしゃに乗のったことないなあ。
Now that I look back, I don’t think I ever rode a tricycle when I was little.

40. 昨年さくねんは1いち輪りんしか開花かいかしなかった。
Not one flower bloomed last year.

～対

The counter ～対つい counts two things that make up a set. Be aware that some kinds of sets like footwear or teams are still counted with their unique counters and thus would not otherwise be counted with ～対.


41. ウサギは切歯せっしを6ろっ本ほん（上左右うえさゆう2に対つい・下左右したさゆう1いっ対つい）持もっている。
Rabbits have six incisors (two pairs to the left and right on the top and one pair to the left and right on the bottom).

42. 細胞さいぼうは父親ちちおや、母親由来ははおやゆらいの染色体せんしょくたいを1ひとつずつ、1いっ対持ついもっている。
Cells carry one pair of chromosomes, one derived from both one’s father and mother.

43. 犬歯けんしは繊維せんいの多おおいものを引ひき裂さくための歯はで上下じょうげ1いっ対ついずつ4よん本ほんあります。
Canines are teeth meant for slicing fibrous foods and there are four of them, a pair both top and both.

44. 脳神経のうしんけい12じゅうに対ついのうち脳幹部のうかんぶより出でる脳神経のうしんけいは10じゅっ対ついあります。
Of the twelve pairs of cranial nerves, there are ten pairs of cranial nerves that come out from the brain stem.

～男

The counter ～男なん counts sons and is typically used only up till five. The phrases for “first son” and “second son” are set phrases. However, when used to just count offspring in general, 1 and 2 are expressed as usual.


45. 無職むしょくの42よんじゅう歳さいの男性だんせいが、就寝中しゅうしんちゅうの22にじゅうに歳さいの長男ちょうなんを斧おので殺害さつがいしようとした疑うたがいで逮捕たいほされた。
An unemployed male aged 42 was arrested under the suspicion that he attempted to murder his eldest son, aged 22, with an axe as he slept.

46. 私わたしは何故なぜ、次男じなんを愛あいせないのか、毎日まいにちそのことを考かんがえて葛藤かっとうしていました。
I have thought and been conflicted every day about why it is that I can’t love my second son.

47. 長男ちょうなん、次男じなんとも家いえを継つぐとかそういうことしたくないと言いい、三男さんなんが跡継あとつぎになりました。
The eldest and second son both stated that they didn’t want to be the successor of the family or anything of the such, and so the third son became the successor.

～女
The counter ～女じょ counts daughters and is typically used only up till five. The phrases for “first daughter” and “second daughter” are set phrases. However, when used to just count offspring in general, 1 and 2 are expressed as usual.


48. 私わたしは夫おっとから長女ちょうじょのDNAディーエヌエイ鑑定かんていを求もとめられました。
I was asked by my husband to have a DNA test done for our eldest daughter.49. 私わたしは長女ちょうじょが5ご歳さいのときに次女じじょを産うみました。
I gave birth to my second daughter when my eldest daughter was five years old.

50. 家族かぞくは妻つまとの間あいだに1いち男なん3さん女じょがいます。
The family has one son and three daughters between him and his wife.
