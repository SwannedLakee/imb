Direction Intransitives + を: 向く

Direction Intransitives + を: 向く

There are a handful of verbs in Japanese that involve directionality. Of these, the first verbs that come to mind are words like 行く(to go), 来る (to come), 歩く (to walk), and 走る (to run). The particles that are naturally associated with these verbs are に and へ, but most intrinsically に. The use of these particles indicate that these verbs are 自動詞  (intransitive verbs) as opposed to 他動詞 (transitive verbs).

However, it is not the case that all direction verbs are intransitive. For example, consider the following verbs:

・指す (to point)                                       

1. コンパスの指針ししんが北西ほくせいを指さした。
The compass needle pointed northwest.

・指差す (to point at)

2. 彼女かのじょは玄関先げんかんさきに置おかれた花瓶かびんを指差ゆびさした。
She pointed at the vase placed at the front door.

・見る (to see/look)

3. ちゃんと前まえを見みて注意ちゅういしながら歩あるいてください。
Please walk while paying attention by properly looking in front of you.

・捜す (to search)

4. 公園こうえんの端はじっこのほうを捜さがすと見みつかるかもしれない。
You might find it if you search the edge of the park.

・向ける (to turn towards)
 5. 事実じじつと道理どうりに背せを向むける。
To turn one’s back on facts and logic.

・目指す (to aim/head for)

6. 与党よとうも野党やとうも大筋同おおすじおなじ方向を目指めざしている。
The ruling party and the opposition party are both aiming roughly in the same direction.

While direction verbs are mostly intransitive, several are, in fact, transitive. What this demonstrates is that “direction” can at times be conceptualized as either a direct or an indirect object.

In this lesson, we will learn about how the particle を is used with one intransitive verb of direction in particular: 向く. Although it has the transitive form 向ける, 向く is frequently seen paired with を. The verb itself has a handful of meanings, each presenting clarity as to which particle ought to be used.

～を向く

The verb 向く is one direction verb in particular that seemingly behaves as a transitive verb due to its use with the case particle を despite being intrinsically intransitive. The thought process as to why を is allowed comes from how the action carried out – shift in direction – is not limited to the physical change in orientation in space, but rather, there is often a subjective shift in attitude that goes along with it.

Yet, ～を向く can also be used even with non-volitional agents, which would not intrinsically show such a concurrent subjective shift, which is precisely why 向く appears to behave as a transitive verb with を.

Even so, the only objects 向く may take are exclusively directional nouns like 上 (up)/下 (down). If the object you are trying to use cannot inherently be conceptualized as a direction, it will need to be embellished with ～のほう to provide the sense of “the direction of.”

7. 目めが上うえを向むいている時ときは、考かんがえをまとめている時ときです。
When one’s eyes are facing up, it’s when one is gathering one’s thoughts.

8. 重おもさで扇風機せんぷうきが下したを向むいてしまいます。
The fan faces downward due to its weight.

9. 下したを向むくな。
Don’t look down.

The を seen in Exs. 7-9 can also be interpreted as the を seen with intransitive verbs of movement for the literal sense of “through” said space.

10. ナマケモノは真後まうしろを向むいたまま、木登きのぼりができる。
Sloths can climb trees whilst still facing right behind them.

11. 本殿ほんどのが海うみを向むいて建たっている。
The main shrine is built towards the sea.

12. 医者いしゃのほうを向むいてではなく、患者かんじゃさんのほうを向むいて仕事しごとをしたい。
I want to work turned towards the patients and not towards the physicians.

13. 僕ぼくはふいと笑美子えみこさんのほうを向むいて言葉ことばをかけた。
I abruptly turned toward Emiko and spoke to her. 

14. 黒板こくばんを向むいて聞きくことが中心ちゅうしんの授業じゅぎょうでは、子供達こどもたちの集中しゅうちゅうも長続ながつづきしません。
In classes centered around listening whilst facing the board, the children’s concentration won’t last long either.

The Transitive Verb 向ける

When moving something into a certain direction as opposed to reorienting oneself, however, the direction necessarily becomes an indirect object, as the “something” is the direct object, and for this, we find that the transitive form 向ける is used. Remember that because the object (at times “oneself”) may still be dropped from a sentence, you may see examples like Ex. 17 which make seem as though 向ける is intransitive, but this is not the case.

15. 謙太郎けんたろうが、左ひだりの扇風機せんぷうきを下したに、右みぎの扇風機せんぷうきを右みぎに向むけた。
Kentaro pointed the fan on the left downward and the fan on the right to the right.

16. 人ひとばかり写うつるので、カメラレンズを上うえに向むけた。
Because only people would be in the picture, I pointed the camera lens upward.

17. さあ、現地へ向けて今から出発だ！
Alrighty, we’re heading out now towards the spot!

When you do see ～を向ける seemingly following a direction word, you are actually looking at ～を向く rendered in its potential form.

18. 皆みなが左ひだりを向むいたとき、あなただけ右みぎを向むけるか。
Only you can seem to turn right when everyone has turned to the left?

～に向く

It goes without saying that the original particle that has always been paired with 向く has been に. This make senses as even in English, the word “toward(s)” usually follows “to turn.” The destination/direction of the orientation of the subject is emphasized with the use of に, and consequently, there isn’t an implied active agent. Lastly, the state that に向く describes must be one that will or is ongoing.

19. 新月しんげつの頃ころは、地球ちきゅうの太陽たいように照てらされた面めんが、ほぼ月つきのほうに向むいている。
In a new moon, the side of the Earth that is illuminated by the Sun is roughly pointed towards the Moon.

Sentence Note: In Ex. 18, the side of the Earth illuminated by the sun is not purposely oriented toward the moon. This is a simple fact of observation about a natural phenomenon.

20. 私わたしの関心かんしんはいつも未来みらいに向むいている。
My interests are always directed to the future.

21. 主人公しゅじんこうの足あしの向むくところには炎ほのおが燃もえ広ひろがっていた。
Flames spread where the protagonist’s feed headed.

Grammar Note: The phrase 足が向く, alternatively seen as 足の向く when modifying a noun phrase as is the case in Ex. 20, uses the particle が before 向く only because no direction-noun is stated.

22. 仕事柄しごとがら、本屋ほんやに行いくと、美術びじゅつの本ほんのコーナーに足あしが向むく。
Because of my work, when I go to a book store, my feet head for the fine arts corner.

23. 家いえの向むきが少すこし斜ななめに向むいている。
The aspect of the house is slightly tilted.

There is a usage of ～に向く unique to it, which is to indicate that X is “suited for” a certain roll, in which case it is synonymous with ～に適している.

24. アナウンサーに向むいている人ひとの特徴とくちょうは何なにでしょうか。
What are the characteristics of someone who’s fit for announcing.

25. うちの息子むすこは物書きに向むいていない。
My son is not cut out for writing.

26. 誰だれしも、今いまの仕事しごとで思おもうような結果けっかが出でないと、「自分じぶんはこの仕事しごとに向むいていないのでは」という疑問ぎもんが頭あたまをよぎったりする。
Whenever one doesn’t get the results that one thought at one’s current job, the question as to whether “one is suited for the job” will cross anyone’s mind.

27. 彼かれの体型たいけいは水泳すいえいに向むいている。
His build is suited for swimming.

～へ向く

～へ向く is largely synonymous with the first sense of ～に向く, but it is especially when one wishes to express a change in orientation that is heading away from an original position.

28. 苗木なえぎが太陽たいようの方ほうへ向むいてぐんぐんと成長せいちょうしている。
The saplings are growing steadily toward the sun.

29. 君きみはどっちの方角ほうがくへ足あしが向むくかね。
I wonder which direction you’ll head for.

30. いつも外がいへ向むいている意識いしきを内側うちがわに向むけ、仕事しごとのストレスを和やわらげましょう。
Point your awareness which is always pointed outward inward and alleviate your work stress.

Ex. 30 demonstrates how ～へ向く, and by proxy ～に向く are suitable for when 向く is used with abstract subjects. ～を向く would, in fact, be incorrect. 

Given how ～を向く, ～に向く, ～へ向く, and  ～を（～に）向ける are not the same, it is more than possible for all these combinations to be used in tandem.  

31. 上半身じょうはんしんを右みぎに向むけて、この右みぎに向むいた時間じかんが長ながいほど、インパクトで体からだが正面しょうめんを向むいている時間じかんは一瞬いっしゅんで、素早すばやく左ひだりへ向むいていくというのがポイントです。
The point is to point your upper body to the right, and the longer it’s pointed to the right, the time the body points to the front at impact becomes instantaneous, at which point the body swiftly faces leftward.  

The Intransitive Verb 向かう

The intransitive verb 向かう is a combination of 向くand the archaic auxiliary verb ふ, which was used to express a continuous state much like ～ている does today but with a far more physical connection between the resulting state and the verb itself. 向かう translates as “to face” or “to go towards” with the subtle nuance that once one ““faces/heads toward” X that the state will last for a certain length of time, or that the change in orientation will have a measurable duration.

While it is seen used with either に or へ depending on the distance factor, it is never used with を.

32. 大型おおがたの台風たいふう15じゅうご号ごうが日本にほんへ向むかっている。
Large-scale Typhoon #15 is heading toward Japan.

33. 冬ふゆに向むかっているのに、なんていいお天気てんき！
Although we’re heading to winter, what great weather it is (today)!

34. 入院にゅういんのブッシュ元大統領もとだいとうりょう、快方かいほうに向むかう
Hospitalized Former President Bush Getting Better

The Suffixes ～向き & ～向け

The suffixes ～向き and ～向け are very similar, but there are a few subtle differences that separate them apart.

The Suffix ～向き

The first usage of ～向き is to mean “facing” when attached to literal direction words such as 東 (east) and 南 (south).

35. 食器棚しょっきだなにお皿さらやコップを入はいれる時とき、下向したむきに置おいた方ほうがいいんでしょうか。
Is it best to place themes bottom up when putting up plates and cups in the cupboard?

36. せっかく新あたらしい家いえに住すむのなら、明あかるい南向みなみむきがいい。
If you’re going to go through the trouble of living in a new home, one facing the bright southerly direction would be best.

37. 私わたしのように今いまネガティブな思考しこうをしている方ほうも、前向まえむきになりたいと思おもったことがあるはずです。
People who, like me, think negatively now should have had a moment where they wanted to become positive.

Phrase Note: 前向き literally means “facing forward” and is usually used in the sense of being “positive/proactive.”

The suffix ～向き may also be used in the sense of ～に適した (suited/apt/fit for…). This is also seen in the phrase 向き不向き meaning “being cut out for certain things and not for others.” It is implied that the suitability is naturally so.

38. 安心あんしんして暮くらせる学生向がくせいむきの物件ぶっけんを数多かずおおく扱あつかっています。
We handle a vast number of properties suited for students to be able to live with peace of mind.

39. この小説しょうせつは偶然ぐうぜんにも、日本語にほんごを勉強べんきょうしている外国人向がいこくじんむきでもある。
This novel is coincidentally also suited for foreigners who are studying Japanese.

40. これは韓国人向かんこくじんむきの辛口からくちビールです。
This is a spicy beer suited for Koreans.

41. 仕事しごとに向むき不向ふむきってあるんですか。
Is there such thing as some things being cut or not cut out for you in jobs?

The Suffix ～向け

Similarly, ～向け is used to indicate that something has been tailored towards a targeted group/entity.

42. 初心者向しょしんしゃむけのIMABIの教科書きょうかしょを買かいたい。
 I wish I could buy an IMABI textbook made for beginners.

43. 日本にほんに住すんでいた時ときは、大人向けの教養番組きょうようばんぐみをいつも見みていた。 
When I lived in Japan, I always watched educational programs made for adults.  

44. 菅元総理大臣すげもとそうりだいじんはいくつかの全国向ぜんこくむけテレビ演説えんぜつをしました。
Prime Minister Kan made several nation-wide televised speeches.

～｛を・に｝振り向く

In the sense of “to draw interest in…,” the object of 振り向く is marked with the particle に. In the sense of “to look back at,” however, the object is marked with the particle を.

45. 興味きょうみのあることには振ふり向むいて目めを合あわせますが、興味きょうみのないことには振ふり向むきもしないことは、日常にちじょうでもよくあることです。
Giving attention and making eye contact with things that are not interesting but not giving a bit of attention to things that aren’t interesting is something that often happens in the ordinary.

46. あなたの好すきな人ひとがどんな人ひとに振り向むくのかリサーチしましょう。
Research the kind of person the person you like gives attention to!

47. あの日ひの朝あさ、息子むすこを学校がっこうに送おくり出だしたとき、こちらを振り向むいた顔かおがまさか最後さいごになるとは思おもいもしませんでした。
That morning when I sent off my son to school, I had no idea that the face he gave me when looking back would be his last. 

～｛を・に｝振り返る

In the sense of “to look back at X” from reacting to some sort of stimulus (noise, etc.), the object is marked with the particle に. In the sense of “to think back on,” the object is marked with the particle を.

48. 背後はいごの奇妙きみょうな物音ものおとに振ふり返かえった。
I turned around at the strange sounds in the back.

49. 驚おどろきながら爆発音ばくはつおんに振ふり返かえった。
I turned around surprised at the noise of the explosion.

50. 50ごじゅう年以上ねんいじょうの昔むかしを振ふり返かえる。
To think back on olden times over fifty years ago.

～｛に・を｝注目する

The object of 注目する (to notice) is marked with the particle に, as it is synonymous with the expression ～目を向ける, which makes it clear why it would take に as the object marker. However, because it is semantically similar to verbs like 見る (to see/look), 注視する (to gaze steadily), and  監視する (to monitor), which all take を to mark the object, many speakers do use ～を注目する.

51. 画面左下がめんひだりした｛に・を △｝注目ちゅうもくする。
Notice the bottom left of the screen.

52. 今後こんごどうなるか｛に・ を △｝注目ちゅうもくしてください。
Please pay attention to what becomes of it from now on.

53. 毎日まいにちツイート｛に・を △｝注目ちゅうもくしてください。
Please pay attention to tweets every day.

54. 医療業界いりょうぎょうかいの成なり行ゆきを｛注視ちゅうし・△ 注目ちゅうもく｝する。
To observe the development of the medical care industry.

55. 画面がめんの右下みぎした｛に・を △｝注目ちゅうもくしてないよね。
You aren’t paying attention to the bottom right of the screen, huh.
